What It Is

An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of investments ‚Äî stocks, bonds, or commodities ‚Äî packaged together so you can invest in many assets at once. Instead of picking individual companies, ETFs let you buy an entire market in a single move.

How It Works

ETFs grow wealth by:

  • Diversification: You own many companies at once, lowering risk.
  • Compounding Growth: Dividends and long-term market gains accumulate quietly.
  • Tracking Indexes: Most ETFs follow major market indexes like the S&P 500 or Total Market.
  • Low Fees: ETFs cost far less than mutual funds, keeping more money in your pocket.
